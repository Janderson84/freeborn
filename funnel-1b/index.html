A 72-hour blackout is no longer a hypothetical. It's a matter of when.

Texas 2021. Puerto Rico 2017. The Northeast ice storm that left a million homes dark for two weeks. Grid failures are accelerating — and the average American household is not prepared for anything beyond a six-hour outage.

No power means no heat, no refrigerated food, no phone charging, and no light after dark. For families with young children, elderly parents, or anyone on medical equipment — it means something far more serious.
