The Moment That Matters

The Job Market Has Changed. Your Resume Probably Hasn't.

In 2019, a well-formatted resume with strong experience would reliably move through screening. Recruiters read. Callbacks happened. The process, while imperfect, was human.

That changed. Companies now process thousands of applications through AI scoring systems before any human involvement. These systems are not reading your resume — they are parsing it for specific signals, in a specific format, against a specific scoring model. If your resume wasn't built for this environment, it doesn't matter what you've accomplished.

The professionals finding this hardest are experienced ones. Their resumes are often dense with accomplishment, written in narrative prose, using the vocabulary of their field — and systematically invisible to the algorithms doing the first pass. ResumeProof was built to fix that specific problem.
