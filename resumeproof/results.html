02

Liability #2

Two-Column Layout Causing Critical ATS Parse Failures

What We Found

Your resume uses a side-column layout with skills and contact info placed in a separate left column. This reads well to humans but is frequently parsed incorrectly by ATS systems — contact data merges with work history, skills appear in the wrong section.

Major ATS platforms including Workday, Greenhouse, and Taleo have documented failures with multi-column PDF layouts. The result: your resume may be imported as garbled text, with your name, employer, and title scrambled.

↓

What It Should Look Like

A single-column layout with clearly labeled sections (Work Experience, Education, Skills) in a standard reading order. All content flows top-to-bottom. Headers and section titles use standard terms that ATS field-mappers recognize.
03

What We Found

Your resume opens directly with your most recent job title and employer. There is no summary, positioning statement, or headline to orient the reader before they scan your history.

A recruiter spending 7 seconds on your resume needs to know what you are before they'll read what you've done. Without a clear positioning statement, they impose their own interpretation — which is usually generic. Your 20 years of experience gets flattened to "another operations person."
