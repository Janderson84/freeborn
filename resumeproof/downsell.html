No Problem — Here's How to Update Your LinkedIn Yourself.

The LinkedIn rewrite isn't for everyone. If you'd rather do it yourself, here's what actually moves the needle — from someone who's reviewed thousands of profiles.

Fix #1

Rewrite Your Headline Beyond Your Job Title

Your headline defaults to your current title. Change it to a positioning statement: who you help, how, with what result. Example: "VP Operations • $500M+ manufacturers • Supply chain transformation • $47M cost reductions"

Fix #2

Write Your About Section in First Person

Most About sections read like a resume in prose form. Write it like a human wrote it: "I've spent 18 years..." Lead with the problem you solve for employers, not your job history. End with a call to action.

Fix #3

Match Your Most Recent Three Roles to Your Resume

Once your new resume arrives, copy the quantified bullets from your three most recent roles into LinkedIn. Exact matches between the two documents signal consistency and reduce recruiter skepticism.
