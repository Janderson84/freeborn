Home Defender Blueprint

The 5-layer home defense system for responsible, law-abiding gun owners. Work through each layer in order.

LAYER 1

Perimeter Hardening

  • Reinforce all exterior doors with 3-inch deadbolt screws (not the factory 1-inch)
  • Install door frame reinforcement plates on all entry doors
  • Add door jamb armor to prevent kick-in attacks
  • Secure sliding glass doors with a bar in the track and window pins
  • Trim bushes near windows and entry points (eliminates concealment for intruders)
  • Install motion-sensor lighting at all entry points and garage
  • Check fence gates: add padlocks where needed
LAYER 2

Active Deterrence

  • Security camera placement: front door, back door, driveway, side gates
  • Visible deterrents: camera signage at eye level (front and back)
  • Smart doorbell camera (records, live view, two-way audio)
  • Timer-based interior lighting when away
  • Do NOT advertise valuables, travel plans, or absence on social media
  • Know your neighbors: a neighborhood watch is the most effective deterrent
LAYER 3

Safe Room Protocol

  • Designate a safe room (master bedroom is most common)
  • Reinforce safe room door: solid core, deadbolt, door stop alarm
  • Stock safe room: phone charger, flashlight, water, first aid
  • Every family member knows: "Go to [room] and lock the door"
  • Establish a verbal all-clear phrase (a word only your family knows)
  • Practice the drill: announce "drill" and time the family response
LAYER 4

Firearm Readiness

The 3AM Decision Framework

Ask yourself before an incident: "If I hear glass breaking right now, what do I do first?" If you cannot answer immediately, you need a plan.
  • Bedside access: quick-access safe (biometric or button) within arm's reach
  • Light: weapon-mounted or bedside white light (no searching in the dark)
  • Ammunition: stored with firearm, verified and accessible
  • Know your backdrop: what is behind every target in your home
  • Firearm must be unloaded when children are present unless in a locked safe
  • Run the drill: can you access your firearm in under 6 seconds from sleep?
LAYER 5

After the Incident

  • Call 911 immediately after the threat is stopped
  • State: "I need police and an ambulance. There has been an intruder."
  • Do NOT say "I shot someone" — keep it brief and factual
  • When officers arrive: holster or secure firearm before they enter
  • Say: "I am the homeowner. I will cooperate fully. I want to speak with an attorney first."
  • Document the scene: photos before anything is moved (if safe to do so)
  • Contact your attorney before giving a full statement
