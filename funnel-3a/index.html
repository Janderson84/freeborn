We've outsourced almost every basic survival skill — and we're about to pay the price.

Two generations ago, the average American could fix a fence, preserve meat, start a fire in the rain, and grow a garden. Today, most of us can barely change a tire. We've traded self-sufficiency for convenience — and the bill is coming due.

I'm not predicting the apocalypse. I'm talking about something far more likely: a job loss, a supply chain shock, a medical event, or a long-term power outage. When those moments come, the people with practical skills will adapt. Everyone else will scramble.

The good news: these skills are completely learnable. You don't need to move to a farm. You just need a roadmap.
