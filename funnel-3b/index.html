Billions of pounds of food are wasted every year in American homes. Most of it could have been preserved.

Food preservation is one of the oldest human skills — and one of the most valuable ones being lost. Our great-grandmothers canned, fermented, dried, and root-cellared food as a matter of survival. We've been outsourcing that to the frozen food aisle for 50 years.

Here's the problem: grocery prices are volatile, and the ability to preserve a garden surplus or a bulk meat purchase is a genuine financial superpower. A family that knows how to preserve food can save thousands per year and build real food resilience at the same time.

It's not hard. It's just a skill — one that takes a weekend to learn and rewards you for decades.
